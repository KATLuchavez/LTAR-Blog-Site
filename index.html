Axioms of Communication

To understand the communication set of circumstances and interpret miscommunication before it develops, it is critical to understand the axioms of communication and how they affect the communication process. Paul Watzlawick, a philosopher and communications theorist, proposed five axioms of human communication in the 1960s, which serve as a framework for analyzing human interaction. Why is it important? These methods can help us not only get more knowledge, but also improve our communication,and interpersonal skills. The five axioms of communications are:

  • Axiom 1: One cannot not communicate
  • Axiom 2: There are both content and relationship levels of communication
  • Axiom 3: The nature of a relationship depends on how both parties punctuate the communication sequence
  • Axiom 4: Communication can be both digital and analogical
  • Axiom 5: All communication is either symmetrical or complementary

Communication Styles

There are a number of communication styles that have been studied and exhibited by people. However, the four (4) basic communication styles are the passive, aggressive, passive-aggressive, and assertive.

Assertive Style: people with this style are able to express their thoughts, emotions and needs clearly and honestly. They are aware of their own needs but also of the needs of other people and they try to compromise respectively. They treat both themselves and other people with respect when they communicate.

Passive Style: people with passive style avoid expressing their emotions and they often try to block their thoughts, emotions and wishes. They are doubtful that when doing so they will somehow trigger conflict or bad feelings between them and the people around them.

Aggressive Style: people with aggressive style tend to care about meeting their own needs and goals first, regardless of the needs of other people. They can be verbally aggressive when communicating with other people and they try to control the others. This leaves other people feeling intimidated, hurt or bitter.

Passive-Aggressive Style: people with passive-aggressive style tend to express their emotions, thoughts and needs in an indirect way, and they do not express their anger in a clear way. They can use sarcasm or the silent treatment. This can leave other people confused or feeling like they've been manipulated.

Can teachers and students be friends?

Fostering positive relationships between teachers and students can create classroom environments that are more conducive to learning. Students not only succeed academically, but teachers can guide them to be emotionally and mentally prepared for their future. Therefore, establishing boundaries between teachers and students is essential in their communication.

Teachers and students, in my opinion, can be "friendly" in a respectful way, but they cannot be friends and should have boundaries and limitations on how they interact. This is part of the student-teacher relationship to have complementary communication, which means that one has more authority than the other (a teacher is of a higher position than that of the student) to also avoid biases within the class. As a result, they should not treat one another equally, as they do with their equal-status friends.
