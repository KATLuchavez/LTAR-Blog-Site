Axioms of Communication Movie Analysis

Four Sisters and a Wedding (2013)

Starring: Angel Locsin, Toni Gonzaga, Bea Alonzo, Shaina Magdayao, and Enchong Dee

Three sisters reunite to dissuade their younger brother from marrying his fiancee. As they interact, they face the feelings and issues they have kept buried for a long time.

Analysis #1

By: Julie Marie Arceli T. Urbina and Jhaycee Anthony P. Acain


    The film revolves around the four Salazar Sisters who reunite for their younger brother’s wedding. However, the sisters are on a quest to dissuade their baby brother CJ from marrying his fiancée, as they disapprove of the prideful and haughty remarks from the fiancée’s family thrown towards their humble home. As the story progresses, the Salazars confront their hidden deep insecurities, issues, and conflicts that they have yet to resolve within their own home.

    Common in most families, typically with siblings, it is not easy to get along with each other’s decisions. This leads to some misunderstandings and cold shoulders when one sibling’s decision is questionable, especially if it concerns their life and future. In one particular scene, CJ walks out on his family when his sisters start to comment on his fiancée's looks, calling her chararat, which is a household expression that translates to ugly. When the sisters notice CJ’s absence at the dinner table, they see him sitting on the swing outside. This type of behavior from CJ dominantly expresses Axiom 1 of Communication which states: One cannot not communicate.

    With CJ’s silent treatment coupled with his glaring, the sisters know something is wrong despite CJ not telling them. In fact, at the swing, CJ only gives them a look that sends a message to each sister to apologize for what they said about his fiancée. Therefore, even in CJ’s best efforts to avoid his sisters, the sisters have already come to their own conclusions of what CJ wants to communicate regarding his feelings about the situation.

Analysis #2

By: Michelle Beatrice J. Po and Erika Anne C. Tumulak


    The movie Four Sisters and A Wedding is a drama-comedy about family relationships. The four sisters, hearing about the wedding of their younger brother, reunite to dissuade their younger brother from marrying his fiancé (whom they don’t approve of). In the midst of their plans to sabotage the wedding, the family, instead are forced by circumstances to face their own shortcomings and misunderstandings.

    Axiom 3: The Nature of a Relationship Depends on How Both Parties Punctuate the Communication Sequence is displayed. Over the course of the movie it is shown that they have numerous (not so) hidden complaints about each other and in this scene it’s all splayed out. Their relationship amongst each other (particularly amongst the sisters) is full of complications because of all the times a family member has done something or said something, the others would internally interpret it in a way that causes them to react negatively, or putting labels and having assumptions on certain behaviors caused by the siblings as well as their mother. This doesn’t only apply to them as a family, but even the way they react to the fiancé of their brother (as well as the fiancé’s family). This is all explicitly shown in the scene at the peak climax of the movie where they finally shed all pretense and have a heart-to-heart talk about all their hurt and misunderstandings over the years amongst themselves. Bea, one of the four sisters, had been hurt all these years, feeling somewhat neglected and loved less (stating that her mother has favorites) due to the numerous instances where her mother seemingly prioritizes her sibling, Teddie, over her, no matter the achievements she seemed to showcase and all the sacrifices she had to make for the betterment of her siblings. She specifies instances where she gets scolded for eating food despite her being hungry, simply because it was meant for the “favorite child”, and not getting praised for the numerous medals she had received in favor of praising Teddie instead despite only getting one medal. Meanwhile Teddie (and the sisters) always had her pegged as arrogant and cold due to her strong facade, whilst the mother never once thought about “favorites” as she stated, “In my heart, I loved you all the same.”
