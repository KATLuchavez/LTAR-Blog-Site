Axioms of Communication Movie Analysis

The Gifted (2014)

Starring: Anne Curtis, Christine Reyes, and Sam Milby

Childhood best friend with a genius IQ become bittersweet rivals when they both fall for Mark.

Analysis

By: Ellen Joy E. Amoin and Kathleen O. Dugang


    The Gifted is a local movie released last September 03, 2014, which was directed by Chris Martinez starring Cristine Reyes as Aica Tabayoyong, Anne Curtis as Zoe Tuazon, and Sam Milby as Mark Ferrer. The movie revolved around the story of two best friends since childhood who are both gifted with intellectual capabilities beyond average yet were not blessed with physical attributes appealing to what was standardized by the society. They eventually became bittersweet rivals for the newly transferred student, Mark Ferrer. Thereafter, Zoe and Aica go through cosmetic surgery and become beautiful vixens engaged in vicious confrontations in an attempt to outsmart and surpass each other.

    Montes (2021) cited Watzlawick’s Axiom No. 5 which states that communication between human beings is either symmetric or complementary which is dependent on the partners' relationship whether it is based on differences or parity. In connection, this is relatable to what we are accustomed to with regards to being well mannered. Every Filipino's heart and mind is instilled with “respect for others”, especially for those who are older than us. Regardless of our generation or geographic area, we place a great value on this attribute. It has become an emblem of our culture in the Philippines (Shiho, 2017). A scene in the film depicted a situation similar to this, in which Anne Curtis' character, Zoe, addresses her religion teacher about her ideas on the biblical genesis of the universe. It all started with a simple laying out of viewpoints in which she opposed all of the teacher's explanations until the conversation turned heated, at which point Zoe responded to the teacher in a provocative manner, infringing the extremely essential trait – “respect”.

    Moreover, it is observable that although Zoe disagreed with the idea taught by her teacher, she still did lower her voice while the nun started to raise hers. This shows that her teacher has a higher power / position than Zoe, dominating her idea while disregarding Zoe's. Furthermore, this was supported by the second part of the scene wherein Cristine Reyes’ character, Aica, whose parents reminded her not to imitate what Zoe did, saying that it is wrong to talk back to elderly. This is hence an example of a complementary relationship.
